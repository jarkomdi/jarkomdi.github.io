What is Israel Independence Day?

Israel Independence Day, also known as Yom Ha'atzmaut in Hebrew, is the national day of Israel. It commemorates the establishment of the modern State of Israel on May 14, 1948. It is a day of celebration and reflection, marking the country's independence from British rule.

On this day, Israelis come together to celebrate their freedom and express their gratitude for the establishment of the Jewish homeland. The day is filled with various festivities, including parades, concerts, fireworks, and family gatherings. It is a time for people to reflect on the significance of Israel as a Jewish state and the challenges it has overcome.

The History and Myth of Israel Independence Day

The history of Israel Independence Day dates back to the early 20th century, when the Zionist movement sought to establish a Jewish homeland in Palestine. The movement gained momentum following the Balfour Declaration of 1917, which expressed British support for the establishment of a Jewish homeland in Palestine.

However, it was not until after World War II and the horrors of the Holocaust that the international community recognized the need for a Jewish state. The United Nations General Assembly passed the partition plan in 1947, which called for the creation of separate Jewish and Arab states in Palestine. This decision paved the way for the establishment of the State of Israel on May 14, 1948.

As for the myth of Israel Independence Day, it is often associated with the biblical story of the Exodus, in which the Jewish people were liberated from slavery in Egypt and led to the Promised Land by Moses.
