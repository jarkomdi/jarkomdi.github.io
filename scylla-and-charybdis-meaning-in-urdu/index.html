What is Scylla and Charybdis Meaning in Urdu?

Scylla and Charybdis, when translated into Urdu, mean "سکاء اور چاریبدس کا مطلب". The term Scylla represents a six-headed sea monster, while Charybdis refers to a whirlpool. Together, they symbolize two dangerous obstacles that sailors in Greek mythology had to navigate through.

In Greek mythology, Scylla was a creature with six heads, each with three rows of sharp teeth. She resided on one side of a narrow strait and would devour any sailors who came too close. On the other side of the strait was Charybdis, a massive whirlpool that would swallow ships whole. Sailors had to carefully navigate between these two perils, often facing a lose-lose situation.

The History and Myth of Scylla and Charybdis Meaning in Urdu

The story of Scylla and Charybdis originates from Greek mythology. According to the myth, Scylla was once a beautiful nymph who caught the attention of the sea god Glaucus. However, the sea witch Circe, who was jealous of Scylla, turned her into a monster. Scylla's transformation led to her transformation into a sea monster with six heads and twelve legs.

Charybdis, on the other hand, was a daughter of Poseidon, the god of the sea. She was punished by Zeus and transformed into a whirlpool after stealing Hercules' cattle. This punishment made Charybdis a relentless and dangerous force of nature.
