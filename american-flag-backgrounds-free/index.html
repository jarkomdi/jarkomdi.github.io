The History and Myth of American Flag Backgrounds Free

The American flag has a rich history and is a symbol of the United States of America. The flag consists of thirteen equal horizontal stripes of red (top and bottom) alternating with white, with a blue rectangle in the canton that contains fifty small, white, five-pointed stars arranged in nine offset horizontal rows, where rows of six stars (top and bottom) alternate with rows of five stars.

The design of the American flag has gone through several changes over the years. The current design, commonly referred to as the "stars and stripes," was adopted on July 4, 1960, and has remained unchanged since then.

There are also several myths and legends associated with the American flag. One of the most well-known myths is the story of Betsy Ross, who is believed to have sewn the first American flag. According to legend, George Washington, Robert Morris, and George Ross visited Betsy Ross in Philadelphia in 1776 and asked her to create a flag for the new nation. Betsy Ross allegedly suggested some changes to the design, such as using five-pointed stars instead of six-pointed ones, and the rest is history.

While the story of Betsy Ross is widely known, there is little historical evidence to support the claim that she was the creator of the first American flag. Nevertheless, the myth has persisted and has become a part of American folklore.
