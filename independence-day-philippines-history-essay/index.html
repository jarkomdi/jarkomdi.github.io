History and Myth of Independence Day Philippines History Essay

The history of Independence Day in the Philippines is rooted in the struggle against Spanish colonial rule. The Filipino people fought bravely for their independence, led by national heroes such as Jose Rizal and Andres Bonifacio. Their courage and sacrifices paved the way for the eventual establishment of the First Philippine Republic on June 12, 1898.

However, there are also myths and legends surrounding the history of Independence Day. One popular myth is that the Filipino flag was first raised in Kawit, Cavite on June 12, 1898. While this story adds a romantic element to the celebration, historical records indicate that the flag was actually first raised in Alapan, Imus, Cavite on May 28, 1898.
