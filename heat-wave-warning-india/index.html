Heat Wave Warning India: History and Myth

Heat waves have been a recurring phenomenon in India for centuries. The country's geographical location and climate make it prone to extreme heat conditions, especially during the summer months. In ancient times, people believed that heat waves were caused by the anger of the sun god and performed rituals to appease him. While these beliefs may be rooted in mythology, the reality is that heat waves are a natural occurrence caused by a combination of factors such as high-pressure systems, lack of rainfall, and global warming.

Over the years, India has witnessed several devastating heat waves that have claimed numerous lives. The 1998 heat wave in Rajasthan and Gujarat, which resulted in over 2,500 deaths, serves as a grim reminder of the dangers associated with extreme heat conditions. Since then, the Indian Meteorological Department has been actively issuing heat wave warnings to prevent such tragedies and minimize the impact of heat waves on the population.
