Understanding the Signs and Symptoms of Heat-Related Illnesses

Heat-related illnesses can range from mild to severe, and it's important to be able to recognize the signs and symptoms. Mild heat exhaustion may present with symptoms such as heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, and nausea. If left untreated, heat exhaustion can progress to heat stroke, which is a medical emergency. Signs of heat stroke include a high body temperature, altered mental state, rapid heartbeat, and flushed skin. If you or someone you know is experiencing symptoms of heat stroke, seek medical attention immediately.
