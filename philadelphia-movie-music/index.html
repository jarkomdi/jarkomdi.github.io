What is Philadelphia Movie Music?

Philadelphia movie music refers to the soundtrack and original score composed for the movie "Philadelphia," directed by Jonathan Demme and released in 1993. The film tells the story of a lawyer, played by Tom Hanks, who is wrongfully terminated from his job after contracting HIV. The music in the movie plays a crucial role in conveying the emotions and themes of the story, making it an integral part of the overall cinematic experience.

The History and Myth of Philadelphia Movie Music

The music for the movie "Philadelphia" was composed by Howard Shore, a renowned film composer known for his work on films like "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy. Shore's hauntingly beautiful score perfectly captures the somber and emotional tone of the film, earning him an Academy Award nomination for Best Original Score. The soundtrack also features songs by various artists, including Bruce Springsteen's iconic song "Streets of Philadelphia," which won the Academy Award for Best Original Song.
