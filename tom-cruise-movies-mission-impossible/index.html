The History and Myth of Tom Cruise movies Mission Impossible

Tom Cruise movies Mission Impossible have a rich history and have become a cultural phenomenon. The franchise first began in 1996 with the release of the first Mission Impossible film, directed by Brian De Palma. The movie was a critical and commercial success, establishing Tom Cruise as an action star and laying the foundation for a successful film series.

Over the years, the Mission Impossible movies have become known for their high-quality action sequences and intricate plots. Each film builds upon the previous ones, creating a cohesive and engaging narrative. The franchise has also introduced memorable characters, such as Luther Stickell (played by Ving Rhames) and Benji Dunn (played by Simon Pegg), who have become fan favorites.

One of the myths surrounding the Mission Impossible franchise is the belief that Tom Cruise performs all of his own stunts. While it is true that Cruise is known for his dedication to performing dangerous stunts, there are instances where stunt doubles are used for safety reasons. However, Cruise's commitment to realism and authenticity is evident in the intense and breathtaking action sequences that are a hallmark of the franchise.

The Mission Impossible movies have also had a significant impact on popular culture. The iconic theme music, composed by Lalo Schifrin, has become synonymous with the franchise and is instantly recognizable to fans. The movies have also inspired countless parodies, tributes, and references in other films and television shows.

In conclusion, the history and myth surrounding Tom Cruise movies Mission Impossible add to the allure and excitement of the franchise. From its humble beginnings in 1996 to its status as a cultural phenomenon, the Mission Impossible movies have left a lasting impact on the world of action cinema.
