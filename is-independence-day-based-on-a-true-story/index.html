Is Independence Day Based on a True Story?

Yes, Independence Day is based on a true story. On July 4, 1776, the Continental Congress declared that the thirteen American colonies were no longer subject to British rule and were now united, free, and independent states. This declaration, known as the Declaration of Independence, marked the birth of the United States of America.

The History and Myth of Independence Day

The history of Independence Day dates back to the American Revolution, when the colonists were fighting for their independence from Britain. The myth surrounding the holiday is that it was celebrated with fireworks by the Founding Fathers immediately after signing the Declaration of Independence. While this is a popular belief, it is not entirely accurate. The first Independence Day celebration took place on July 8, 1776, in Philadelphia, with a reading of the Declaration of Independence and a parade. Fireworks were not a part of the early celebrations, but they became a tradition in the years that followed.

The Hidden Secrets of Independence Day

While Independence Day is widely celebrated and known for its fireworks, barbecues, and parades, there are some hidden secrets about the holiday. One of these secrets is that the Second Continental Congress actually voted to declare independence on July 2, 1776, not July 4. However, it was on July 4 that the final wording of the Declaration of Independence was approved and the document was officially adopted.
